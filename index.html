Problem Statement

The gap: Young roommates and siblings want to check in on each other’s mood without staring at phones or typing long texts.

The opportunity: A calm, physical “mood totem” on a shared shelf could make emotional check-ins feel playful and low-pressure—like leaving a sticky note, but softer.

Why it matters: Screens add cognitive load; a tangible ritual (stack, squeeze, tap) can make care feel more like play and less like homework.

✦

Conceptual Design

Whisper Blocks are foam-covered cubes with pressure sensors. Each face is a “feeling color.” Stack them to send a quiet signal to a paired block across the room: lights and gentle haptics echo your stack.

What makes it novel

  • Emotion is encoded in order and squish, not buttons or menus.
  • Paired blocks give ambient feedback—no inbox, no notifications list.
  • Soft materials invite fidgeting, which doubles as input.
